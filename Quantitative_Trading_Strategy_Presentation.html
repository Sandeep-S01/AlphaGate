Trend Following (Multi-Timeframe)

15% - 35%

Academic Rationale
Markets exhibit momentum at intermediate horizons (3-12 months) due to slow information diffusion. Online gradient-based models achieve historical Sharpe ratios of 1.26 with proper regularization.
When It Works Best
Strong macro directional markets, post-breakout continuations, high volatility trends.
When It Fails
Choppy range-bound sideways markets, sharp V-shaped reversals, extremely low volatility environments.

//@version=5
strategy("Trend Following System", overlay=true, default_qty_type=strategy.percent_of_equity, default_qty_value=10)

fastLength = input.int(20, "Fast EMA Length")
slowLength = input.int(50, "Slow SMA Length")
adxLength = input.int(14, "ADX Length")
adxThreshold = input.float(25.0, "ADX Threshold")
volMultiplier = input.float(1.5, "Volume Multiplier")
atrLength = input.int(14, "ATR Length")

emaFast = ta.ema(close, fastLength)
smaSlow = ta.sma(close, slowLength)
adxValue = ta.adx(high, low, close, adxLength)
atrValue = ta.atr(atrLength)
volumeSMA = ta.sma(volume, 20)
highest20 = ta.highest(high, 20)

trendUp = close > emaFast and close > smaSlow
trendStrength = adxValue > adxThreshold
volumeConfirm = volume > volumeSMA * volMultiplier
breakout = close > highest20[1]

trendDown = close < emaFast or close < smaSlow
adxWeak = adxValue < 20

riskAmount = strategy.equity * riskPerTrade
stopDistance = atrValue * 2
qty = riskAmount / stopDistance

if trendUp and trendStrength and volumeConfirm and breakout and strategy.position_size == 0
    strategy.entry("Long", strategy.long, qty=qty)
    strategy.exit("Exit Long", "Long", stop=close - atrValue * 2, trail_points=atrValue * 10, trail_offset=atrValue * 3)

if trendDown and adxWeak and strategy.position_size > 0
    strategy.close("Long", comment="Trend Reversal")

Pine Script v5 (Momentum Breakout) 
//@version=5
strategy("Momentum Breakout", overlay=true)

lookback = input.int(20, "Breakout Lookback")
rsiLength = input.int(14, "RSI Length")
bbLength = input.int(20, "BB Length")
volMult = input.float(2.0, "Volume Multiplier")
riskPct = input.float(1.0, "Risk %") / 100

resistance = ta.highest(high, lookback)[1]
support = ta.lowest(low, lookback)[1]
rsi = ta.rsi(close, rsiLength)
basis = ta.sma(close, bbLength)
dev = 2 * ta.stdev(close, bbLength)
upper = basis + dev
lower = basis - dev
bbWidth = (upper - lower) / basis
volAvg = ta.sma(volume, 20)

breakout = close > resistance
volumeConfirm = volume > volAvg * volMult
rsiFilter = rsi > 50 and rsi < 70
volatilityExpand = bbWidth > 0.06

longCondition = breakout and volumeConfirm and rsiFilter and volatilityExpand

if longCondition and strategy.position_size == 0
    stopDist = close - support
    tpDist = stopDist * 2
    qty = (strategy.equity * riskPct) / stopDist
    strategy.entry("BreakoutLong", strategy.long, qty=qty)
    strategy.exit("TP/SL", "BreakoutLong", limit=close + tpDist, stop=support)
Strategy 3

Mean Reversion (Adaptive Confidence-Weighted)

Statistical High Win Rate
20% - 50%
Expected Return
12% - 25%
Max Drawdown
55% - 70%
Win Rate
1.75
Sharpe Ratio
Market Behavior Exploited
Short-term price extremes driven by dealer hedging, liquidity shortages, and stop-loss hunts that quickly revert back to historical averages.
Why the Edge Exists
Market makers and algos temporarily push prices beyond fair value to clear order books. Once volume dries up, mean reversion occurs. Confidence Weighted Mean Reversion (CWMR) models achieve high Sharpe ratios (~1.75) and massive compounding gains.
When It Works Best
Range-bound sideways periods, post-volatile exhaustion crashes, and liquid high-beta assets.
When It Fails
Strong structural trending break markets, black swan liquidations, and major regime shifts.
BUY Signal Conditions
  • Price falls below the Lower Bollinger Band (20, 2.5 SD)
  • RSI(2) < 10 (extreme short-term oversold)
  • Price > SMA(200) (trend filter: only buy pullbacks in long-term uptrends)
  • Volume > 1.5x average (signaling capitulation volume)
SELL / Exit Conditions
  • Price touches the middle Bollinger Band (reversion target)
  • RSI(2) crosses above 50
  • Stop Loss: Close below entry-day low - 0.5x ATR
  • Time Stop: Exit after 3 days regardless of target status
Position Sizing
Inverse-volatility weighting. Scale up positions in low-volatility regimes and scale down when volatility expands.
Risk Limits
Max risk per trade: 0.75%. Max daily loss: 2.0%. Hard stop out at 12% drawdown.
Volatility Filter
Temporarily suspend all new mean reversion signals when VIX > 30 (equities) or equivalent crypto fear/volatility indexes spike.
Backtesting Frame
10+ years covering market cycles, verifying average recovery periods and worst-case drawdown duration.
Pine Script v5 (Adaptive Mean Reversion) 
//@version=5
strategy("Adaptive Mean Reversion", overlay=true)

bbLength = input.int(20, "BB Length")
bbMult = input.float(2.5, "BB Multiplier")
rsiLength = input.int(2, "RSI Length")
trendFilter = input.int(200, "Trend SMA")
riskPct = input.float(0.75, "Risk %") / 100

basis = ta.sma(close, bbLength)
dev = bbMult * ta.stdev(close, bbLength)
upper = basis + dev
lower = basis - dev
rsi2 = ta.rsi(close, rsiLength)
sma200 = ta.sma(close, trendFilter)
atr = ta.atr(14)
volAvg = ta.sma(volume, 20)

oversold = close < lower
extremeRSI = rsi2 < 10
trendUp = close > sma200
capitulation = volume > volAvg * 1.5

longCondition = oversold and extremeRSI and trendUp and capitulation

if longCondition and strategy.position_size == 0
    stopPrice = low - atr * 0.5
    targetPrice = basis
    riskAmt = close - stopPrice
    qty = (strategy.equity * riskPct) / riskAmt
    strategy.entry("MR_Long", strategy.long, qty=qty)
    strategy.exit("MR_Exit", "MR_Long", limit=targetPrice, stop=stopPrice)

if strategy.position_size > 0 and bar_index - strategy.closedtrades.exit_bar_index(strategy.closedtrades - 1) > 3
    strategy.close("MR_Long", comment="Time Stop")

Statistical Arbitrage (Pairs Trading)

Market Neutral Low Beta
10% - 25%
Expected Return
8% - 18%
Max Drawdown
60% - 75%
Win Rate
5 - 20 / mo
Trade Frequency
Market Behavior Exploited
Temporary pricing deviations between cointegrated pairs (e.g. BTC-ETH, NIFTY-BANKNIFTY) from their long-term equilibrium.
Why the Edge Exists
Securities tied to common fundamentals and risk exposures must share statistical relationships; non-fundamental flows cause short-term cointegration breakdowns that quickly self-correct.
When It Works Best
Stable market regimes, high sector liquidity, and low transaction-cost platforms.
When It Fails
Structural breaks, corporate events/delistings, and systemic liquidity flight across exchanges.
Spread Entry Conditions
  • Z-score of pricing ratio > +2.0 (under/over performer divergence)
  • Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) test p-value < 0.05 (confirming cointegration)
  • Estimated half-life of mean reversion < 20 days
Spread Exit Conditions
  • Z-score returns to target mean area (0.5)
  • Stop Loss: Z-score expands beyond ±3.5
  • Time Stop: Exit if held longer than 2.0x of the estimated half-life
Position Sizing
Dollar-neutral. Maintain identical notional capital allocations on both long and short legs of the pair.
Risk Controls
Max risk per spread trade: 1.0% of capital. Max daily drawdown: 2.0%. Hard stop out at 15%.
Model Maintenance
Recalculate all cointegration matrices, residuals, and half-lives on a rolling monthly interval.
Backtesting Frame
5+ years covering diverse correlation regimes and pricing divergence history.
Pine Script v5 (Z-Score Indicator for Pairs) 
//@version=5
strategy("Pairs Trading - Z-Score", overlay=false)

lookback = input.int(60, "Lookback Period")
entryZ = input.float(2.0, "Entry Z-Score")
exitZ = input.float(0.5, "Exit Z-Score")
stopZ = input.float(3.5, "Stop Z-Score")
assetB = input.symbol("BINANCE:ETHUSDT", "Asset B")

[openB, highB, lowB, closeB] = request.security(assetB, timeframe.period, [open, high, low, close])

spread = close / closeB
spreadSMA = ta.sma(spread, lookback)
spreadSTD = ta.stdev(spread, lookback)
zScore = (spread - spreadSMA) / spreadSTD

longSpread = zScore < -entryZ
shortSpread = zScore > entryZ
exitLong = zScore > -exitZ or zScore < -stopZ
exitShort = zScore < exitZ or zScore > stopZ

plot(zScore, "Z-Score", color=color.blue)
hline(entryZ, "Entry+", color=color.red)
hline(-entryZ, "Entry-", color=color.green)
hline(0, "Mean", color=color.gray)

VWAP Reversion (Institutional Flow)

Intraday Equities Focus
15% - 35%
Expected Return
8% - 15%
Max Drawdown
55% - 65%
Win Rate
1 - 3 / day
Trade Frequency
Market Behavior Exploited
Intraday over-extensions from the Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP) driven by large institutional execution programs.
Why the Edge Exists
Institutions executing large blocks push prices away from VWAP due to lack of immediate liquidity. Once execution pauses, arbitrageurs revert the price back. Especially reliable in Indian Equities (NIFTY/BANKNIFTY).
When It Works Best
High-volume non-trending sessions, large-cap liquid equities.
When It Fails
Strong directional "trend days", major gap-up/gap-down openings, low liquidity periods.
BUY Signal Conditions
  • Price falls below VWAP - 0.3x Standard Deviation(VWAP)
  • RSI(14) < 40
  • Volume > 1.2x average (indicating institutional block activity)
  • Current time > 10:15 AM (after initial open range settles)
SELL / Exit Conditions
  • Price crosses above the VWAP line
  • RSI(14) > 60
  • Current time > 3:15 PM (intraday cut-off)
  • Stop Loss: VWAP - 1.0x SD(VWAP)
Position Sizing
Fixed notional allocation per equity trade to manage institutional fill risks.
Risk Limits
Max risk per trade: 0.5%. Max daily loss: 2.0%. Portfolio drawdown halt: 10%.
Execution Controls
Intraday square-off strictly enforced at 3:15 PM (NSE) or equivalent session boundary. No overnight carry.
Backtesting Frame
3+ years of high-resolution intraday historical data with realistic slippage estimates.
Pine Script v5 (VWAP Reversion) 
//@version=5
strategy("VWAP Reversion", overlay=true)

vwapDev = input.float(0.3, "VWAP Deviation Entry")
rsiLength = input.int(14, "RSI Length")
rsiEntry = input.int(40, "RSI Entry Threshold")
timeStart = input.time("1015", "Start Time")
timeEnd = input.time("1515", "End Time")
riskPct = input.float(0.5, "Risk %") / 100

vwapValue = ta.vwap(hlc3)
vwapDevValue = ta.stdev(close - vwapValue, 20)
rsi = ta.rsi(close, rsiLength)
volAvg = ta.sma(volume, 20)

timeAllowed = time >= timeStart and time <= timeEnd
belowVWAP = close < vwapValue - vwapDevValue * vwapDev
oversold = rsi < rsiEntry
volumeOK = volume > volAvg * 1.2

longCondition = belowVWAP and oversold and volumeOK and timeAllowed

if longCondition and strategy.position_size == 0
    stopPrice = vwapValue - vwapDevValue
    qty = (strategy.equity * riskPct) / (close - stopPrice)
    strategy.entry("VWAP_Long", strategy.long, qty=qty)
    strategy.exit("VWAP_Exit", "VWAP_Long", limit=vwapValue, stop=stopPrice)

if time > timeEnd and strategy.position_size > 0
    strategy.close("VWAP_Long", comment="Session End")
Strategy 6

Market Making (Crypto-Focused)

High Frequency Inventory Hedged
15% - 40%
Expected Return
5% - 12%
Max Drawdown
60% - 80%
Win Rate (per RT)
100 - 1000+
Trades / Day
Market Behavior Exploited
Capturing the bid-ask spread and optimizing skew parameters to hedge asset inventory variance in highly liquid markets.
Why the Edge Exists
Persistent retail order flow creates continuous execution spreads (0.01% - 0.10%). By providing liquidity to the order book, market makers capture these spreads while managing positional inventory risks.
When It Works Best
High volume, sideways range-bound conditions on liquid primary pairs (e.g. BTC-USDT, ETH-USDT).
When It Fails
Extreme breakout trends (volatility spikes), adverse selection (toxic flow), exchange API halts or sudden latency issues.
Order Placement Triggers
  • Bid-Ask Spread > minimum threshold limit (0.02%)
  • Inventory skew is below 50% of the maximum allowed capacity
  • Volatility (ATR / price) is low (< 2.0%)
  • Perpetual funding rate is neutral (within ±0.01%)
Cancellation & Adjustments
  • Inventory skew rises above 70% (forces aggressive skew modification)
  • ATR volatility expands beyond 3.0%
  • Perpetual funding rate goes above 0.05% (adverse carry)
  • Round-trip order execution latency exceeds 50ms
Inventory Management
Max allocation limit: 20% of capital in any single asset. Skew order prices to encourage inventory reduction when net delta accumulates.
Trade Protection
Max risk per filled round-trip: 0.1%. Max daily loss: 1.0%. Drawdown threshold halt: 8.0%.
Adverse Selection Filter
Integrate order book imbalance and toxic flow markers (VPIN). Pause quotes if aggressive buyer/seller imbalance exceeds 75%.
Backtesting Frame
1+ years using high-frequency tick data containing full order book L2/L3 dynamics.

Strategy 7

Funding Rate Arbitrage (Crypto)

Yield Delta Neutral
10% - 25%
Expected Return
3% - 8%
Max Drawdown
90%+
Win Rate (per payment)
3 / day
Trade Frequency
Market Behavior Exploited
Capturing yield differentials between perpetual futures and underlying spot prices during speculative periods.
Why the Edge Exists
Perpetual funding rates adjust every 8 hours to force futures prices to track spot prices. Bullish speculation pushes funding rates extremely high (often > 30% annualized), enabling risk-free yield capture.
When It Works Best
High leverage directional speculation, extremely bullish or bearish funding regimes.
When It Fails
Funding rate reversals, exchange counterparty failures, platform borrow rate increases.
Arbitrage Entry Rules
  • Funding rate > 0.10% per 8h period (or < -0.10% for short arbitrage)
  • Spot-to-Perp basis spreads exceed 0.05%
  • Borrow rates (if shorting spot) are low enough to preserve margins
Hedge Execution
  • Positive Funding: Long Spot Asset + Short Perp Future
  • Negative Funding: Short Spot Asset + Long Perp Future
  • Exit: Close spread when funding rate falls below 0.02% per 8h
Position Sizing
Maintain equal notional sizes on both spot and futures legs to protect against delta exposure.
Risk Protections
Max risk per trade: 0.5% (exchange custody default). Max daily loss: 1.0%. Target max drawdown constraint: 5.0%.
Liquidation Guard
Monitor margin ratios dynamically. Rebalance collaterals to protect futures leg from sudden mark-price spikes.
Backtesting Frame
2+ years of historical funding logs across multiple global exchange platforms.
Arbitrage Execution Logic (Pseudocode) 
for exchange in exchanges:
    funding_rate = get_current_funding(exchange)
    basis = perp_price - spot_price
    
    if funding_rate > threshold_limit and basis > minimum_basis:
        # positive funding rate arbitrage entry
        spot_order_id = execute_buy_spot(size)
        perp_order_id = execute_sell_perp(size, leverage=1)
        
        monitor_position_until_payment()
        
    elif funding_rate < -threshold_limit and basis < -minimum_basis:
        # negative funding rate arbitrage entry
        spot_order_id = execute_short_spot(size)
        perp_order_id = execute_buy_perp(size, leverage=1)
Strategy 8

Grid Trading (Range-Bound Markets)

Non-Directional Crypto Focus
15% - 40%
Expected Return
15% - 30%
Max Drawdown
50% - 60%
Win Rate (per Grid)
10 - 100+
Trades / Day
Market Behavior Exploited
Persistent price oscillations within fixed horizontal trading channels in consolidating markets.
Why the Edge Exists
Crypto markets spend 60-70% of their time consolidations rather than trends. Grid trading automates buying low and selling high at defined price intervals within this noise.
When It Works Best
High volatility range-bound sideways markets.
When It Fails
Strong directional trends (leading to inventory skew build-up and loss), exchange fee structures eroding margin.
Setup Rules
  • Identify channel range bounds (typically ±10% around mid-price)
  • Place 10-20 equidistant grid purchase levels
  • Buy orders queued below current market price; sell orders queued above
Rebalance Executions
  • When a limit buy is filled, immediately post a limit sell one grid size above
  • When a limit sell is filled, post a limit buy one grid size below
  • Shift the channel center if price drifts > 15% from the original midpoint
Position Sizing
Equal capital allocation distributed across each grid level.
Risk Protections
Max risk per grid level: 0.5%. Max daily loss: 3.0%. Global stop out: 20% drawdown.
Grid Shift Management
If prices break clean out of the horizontal bounds, close all open grid items immediately to avoid holding toxic inventory.
Backtesting Frame
2+ years covering volatile and trending regimes.
Grid Trading Algorithm (Pseudocode) 
grid_levels = 20
grid_size = (range_upper - range_lower) / grid_levels

for i in range(grid_levels):
    price_target = range_lower + i * grid_size
    if current_price > price_target:
        queue_limit_buy(price_target, size_per_level)
    else:
        queue_limit_sell(price_target, size_per_level)
        
# Event Loop on execution
on_order_fill(filled_order):
    if filled_order.side == "BUY":
        queue_limit_sell(filled_order.price + grid_size, size)
    elif filled_order.side == "SELL":
        queue_limit_buy(filled_order.price - grid_size, size)

Multi-Factor Momentum (AI-Enhanced)

Ensemble Quant & AI
25% - 55%
Expected Return
15% - 25%
Max Drawdown

Sharpe Ratio
Market Behavior Exploited
Composite alignment of trend momentum, liquidity expansion, and low-volatility regimes across multi-asset categories.
Why the Edge Exists

When It Works Best
Dynamic trends, high liquid sectors, and periods of clear risk-on flow.
When It Fails
Sudden macro regime rotations, factor crowding, and out-of-sample overfitting structures.
BUY Rules (Requires >= 5/6 Score)

  • RSI(14) is between 50 and 70 (not overbought)
  • MACD Histogram > 0 and expanding
  • ATR(14) / Close < 3.0% (volatility filter: avoid high-noise zones)
  • Volume > 1.3x average volume
SELL / Exit Conditions
  • Overall composite score drops below 3.0
  • Trailing Stop-Loss: set at 2.5x ATR
  • Time Stop: Exit position after 10 days if targets not hit
Position Sizing
Quarter-Kelly Criterion ($0.25 \times f^*$) to account for parameter variance and model errors.
Risk Limits
Max risk per trade: 2.0%. Max daily loss: 4.0%. Hard stop threshold: 18%.
AI Enhancement Path
Incorporate Bi-LSTM structures for signal verification, Gaussian Mixture Models for macro regime detection, and alternative sentiment data.
Backtesting Frame
5-10 years minimum using out-of-sample datasets to mitigate overfitting.

//@version=5

Strategy 10

Smart Money / Order Flow (Advanced)

Microstructure Order Flow
30% - 80%
Expected Return
20% - 35%
Max Drawdown
50% - 60%
Win Rate
3 - 10 / day
Trade Frequency
Market Behavior Exploited
Tracking footprint dynamics left by institutional players: cumulative volume delta (CVD) divergence, order book imbalances, and liquidation cascades.
Why the Edge Exists
Institutions act as the primary liquidity sinks. Tracking the flow imbalances and liquidations allows models to entry before major momentum cascades.
When It Works Best
High volume, liquid trading sessions, major support/resistance zones, and high futures liquidation events.
When It Fails
Low volume conditions, persistent order spoofing, and delayed exchange API data streams.
BUY Signal Conditions
  • CVD registers a positive divergence relative to spot prices
  • Aggressive buy volume imbalances detected (> 5.0x normal block sizes)
  • Futures liquidation cascade completes (Open Interest drops, funding rates drop)
  • Price exhibits liquidity absorption at key support levels
SELL / Exit Conditions
  • CVD registers a negative divergence relative to spot prices
  • Large institutional limit offers block overhead resistance
  • Funding rate rises above positive extremes (> 0.10%)
  • Stop Loss: Set at recent swing low minus 1.0x ATR
Position Sizing
Aggressive scaling when institutional order flow confirms; conservative or flat when signals diverge.
Risk Limits
Max risk per trade: 1.5%. Max daily loss: 3.0%. Hard stop threshold: 15%.
Execution Controls
